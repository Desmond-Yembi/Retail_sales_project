# Retail Sales Analysis Dashboard

## Project Overview

This project analyzes retail sales transaction data to uncover trends in sales performance, customer purchasing behavior, product category performance, discount utilization, and payment preferences. The analysis was conducted using Power BI to transform raw retail data into actionable business insights through interactive dashboards.

---

## Objectives

* Monitor overall sales performance and revenue trends.
* Identify top-performing product categories and items.
* Analyze customer purchasing patterns.
* Evaluate the impact of discounts on sales.
* Understand payment mode preferences.
* Support data-driven business decision-making through interactive visualizations.

---

## Tools Used

* **Power BI** – Dashboard Development & Data Visualization
* **Excel** – Data Cleaning and Preparation
* **DAX** – KPI Calculations and Measures
* **Power Query** – Data Transformation

---

## Dataset Features

The dataset contains the following fields:

* Transaction ID
* Customer ID
* Category
* Item
* Price per Unit
* Quantity
* Total Spent
* Payment Mode
* Transaction Date
* Discount Applied (True/False)

---

## Dashboard Structure

### Page 1: Executive Sales Overview

Key visualizations include:

* Total Sales KPI
* Total Transactions KPI
* Sales Trend Analysis
* Sales by Product Category
* Discount Distribution
* Interactive Year Filter

#### Business Questions Answered

* What is the total revenue generated?
* How many transactions were completed?
* Which categories generate the highest sales?
* How have sales changed over time?
* How frequently are discounts applied?

---

### Page 2: Customer & Product Insights

Key visualizations include:

* Sales by Payment Mode
* Product Performance Analysis
* Customer Purchase Trends
* Discount Impact Analysis
* Revenue Contribution by Category

#### Business Questions Answered

* Which payment methods are most preferred?
* Which products contribute the most revenue?
* What purchasing patterns exist among customers?
* How do discounts influence sales performance?
